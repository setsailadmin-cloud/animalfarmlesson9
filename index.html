Chapter 9 Core Focus

In Chapter 9, the farm faces a brutal winter food crisis. While the rations of the workers are drastically "readjusted" (reduced), the pigs continue to grow fat and establish mandatory "Spontaneous Demonstrations" to simulate prosperity.

Boxer, exhausted by his tireless labor and aged, collapses physically at the quarry. In the ultimate act of betrayal, Napoleon secretly sells him to a horse slaughterer in exchange for money to buy whisky, while Squealer convinces the animals that Boxer died peacefully in a prestigious human hospital.

Historical Parallel: The tragic sacrifice and sale of Boxer represents the Soviet state's final betrayal of its working class (Stakhanovites), exploiting their loyalty to physical death to finance the party bureaucracy.
